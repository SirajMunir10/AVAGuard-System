================================================================================
SECTION 15: PENETRATION TEST REPORTING
================================================================================

The penetration test report is the primary deliverable of an engagement and the means by which technical findings are translated into actionable business decisions. A high-quality report must serve two distinct audiences simultaneously: executive leadership who need to understand business risk without technical detail, and security and development teams who need precise technical information to reproduce and remediate findings.

Every vulnerability finding in the report must follow a standardized structure. The finding must include a unique identifier for tracking purposes, a descriptive title that clearly states the vulnerability type and affected component, a severity rating with the full CVSS v3.1 vector string rather than just a label, the CWE identifier that categorizes the type of weakness, the affected asset including the exact URL or hostname and environment, a clear description of what the vulnerability is and how it works, step-by-step reproduction instructions that are precise enough for another engineer to reproduce the finding without additional guidance, evidence in the form of screenshots or request and response captures that prove the vulnerability was confirmed through exploitation, a description of the realistic business impact if the vulnerability were exploited by a real attacker, specific remediation guidance naming the exact fix rather than a generic category, and references to relevant standards, CVE identifiers, or secure coding guidance.

Reproduction steps must be written to a sufficient level of precision that a skilled security engineer can reproduce the finding from the steps alone. Vague reproduction steps such as perform SQL injection in the login form are not acceptable. Steps should specify the exact URL or endpoint, the exact payload used, the exact tool or manual technique, and the exact indicator that confirms exploitation. If the finding was discovered with automated tooling, the exact command used should be included.

Business impact descriptions must connect the technical finding to concrete organizational consequences. A finding about SQL injection should not conclude its impact description at the ability to extract database records. It should continue to describe that the customer database contains personally identifiable information for the user base, that unauthorized access to this data would trigger breach notification obligations under relevant regulations, that the organization would face regulatory fines and reputational damage, and that the compromised credentials could enable further attacks against customers. Executives who read only the business impact section should understand why the finding matters to the organization.

The executive summary is read by leadership who may not have technical backgrounds and is often the only section they read. It must clearly state what was tested and during what time period, what the overall risk rating of the environment is, the two or three findings that pose the greatest risk to the business in plain language, what could happen to the organization if these findings were exploited by a real attacker, and what the most important remediation actions are. The executive summary should avoid technical terminology and should not exceed two pages.

Remediation guidance must be specific and actionable. Generic guidance such as apply input validation or update your software does not give developers what they need to fix the issue. Guidance should specify the exact coding pattern, configuration change, or patch to apply. For a SQL injection finding, guidance should specify that parameterized queries or prepared statements must be used for all database interactions and provide a code example in the language used by the application. For an outdated software finding, the guidance should specify the exact version to upgrade to and the security release notes that address the finding.
